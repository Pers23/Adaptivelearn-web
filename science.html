Science Quiz

1. What is the primary source of energy for the Earth?

The Sun provides the Earth with light and heat, making it the primary source of energy.

2. What gas do plants use for photosynthesis?

Plants take in carbon dioxide from the air and use it to make food through photosynthesis.

3. What organ pumps blood throughout the body?

The heart is a muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the circulatory system.

4. Which planet is known as the Red Planet?

Mars is often called the Red Planet due to its reddish appearance caused by iron oxide.

5. What is the boiling point of water at sea level?

At sea level, water boils at 100°C.

6. Which of these is a mammal?

Dolphins are mammals because they breathe air and give birth to live young.

7. What part of the plant conducts photosynthesis?

Leaves contain chlorophyll which is used to capture sunlight for photosynthesis.

8. Which system in the body is responsible for transporting blood?

The circulatory system moves blood through the heart, arteries, and veins.

9. Which state of matter has no fixed shape but has a fixed volume?

Liquids have a definite volume but take the shape of their container.

10. Which force pulls objects toward the Earth?

Gravity is the force that attracts objects towards the center of the Earth.

11. What is the main function of the lungs?

The lungs exchange oxygen and carbon dioxide during breathing.

12. What kind of energy does a moving object have?

Kinetic energy is the energy of motion.

13. What type of rock is formed from lava?

Igneous rocks form when lava or magma cools and solidifies.

14. What do we call animals that eat only plants?

Herbivores eat only plants.

15. Which natural phenomenon is measured with the Richter scale?

The Richter scale measures the magnitude of earthquakes.
